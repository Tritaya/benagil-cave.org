From Armação de Pêra

Why leave from Armação de Pêra

Armação de Pêra is the closest town on the eastern side of Benagil, and its long beach — Praia dos Pescadores at the old-town end — launches small boats straight off the sand. It pairs a short crossing with the lowest published fares on the coast, which is why it’s a favourite of value-minded visitors.

It’s also far easier for parking and driving than squeezing into tiny Benagil village, making it a relaxed base for a morning cave trip.

Getting there & parking

The essentials

  • Departure point: Praia dos Pescadores, at the eastern (old-town) end of Armação de Pêra — beach-launched small boats.
  • Time to the cave: ~15–25 min each way by small boat.
  • Driving here: From Faro airport ~45 km / ~40 minutes via the A22 (exit Alcantarilha). From Albufeira ~20 min, from Lagos ~35 min.
  • Parking: Free street and lot parking around town (search “Armação de Pêra beach” car parks) — plentiful most of the year, tighter in July–August. Much easier than Benagil village.

Before You Book

The 2024 access rules apply here too

Wherever you leave from, the same binding rules govern the cave itself: you cannot land on the beach inside, you cannot swim or float in, and motorised boats may only enter for about two minutes. Kayaks and SUPs go in with a guide, in small groups. It's the same cave under the same law — the town only changes how you get there.

Full detail, with the official sources, is on the access-rules section of the main guide. If any Armação de Pêra operator promises a stop on the beach inside Benagil, that's the pre-2024 experience — check before paying.
